## Idea Organization and Prioritization

**Thematic Organization:**

**Product Scope and Guardrails**

- **Commercial-only first product:** Keep the first version focused on clients, quotes, and invoices without drifting into PM, CRM, or ERP territory.
- **Anti-bloat refusal set:** Explicitly refuse task boards, arbitrary custom fields, pricing formula engines, and silent rewriting after approval.
- **Tool, not platform:** Position the product as a lightweight quoting and invoicing tool for creative studios rather than an operations platform.

**Studio Quote Builder Core**

- **Studio Quote Builder:** The product center and primary source of value; all major workflow decisions should strengthen this capability.
- **Template-led quoting:** Quotes should begin from one or more editable templates rather than a blank page.
- **Stacked multi-template sections:** Multiple selected templates should appear as clear stacked sections inside one unified proposal.
- **Complexity-based defaults with light toggles:** Pricing should be driven by simple defaults and a few meaningful modifiers like rush and multi-channel work.
- **Preview-first trust layer:** Users should always preview the polished client-facing quote before sending or marking it accepted.

**Trustworthy Commercial Workflow**

- **Thin client continuity:** Client records should stay lightweight but carry cleanly into quotes and invoices.
- **Preview -> accepted -> convert:** The core workflow arc that makes the demo feel real without building an approval engine.
- **Invoice draft after conversion:** Quote conversion should create an editable invoice draft, not immediately issue an invoice.
- **Partial invoice by selected line items:** Support light real-world billing flexibility without adding a billing-schedule engine.
- **Linked add-on/change-order quotes:** Preserve the approved baseline while handling scope change cleanly.

**Demo and Build Sequencing**

- **Editable template library first:** Templates are the product intelligence and should be built before the builder.
- **Thin but real persisted E2E milestone:** The first milestone should use real saved data for clients, templates, quotes, and invoices.
- **One hero surface:** Make the Studio Quote Builder the deepest product surface and keep support areas intentionally shallow.
- **Safe cuts:** Quick standalone invoice, send/approval workflow, PM/resource features, AI cost tracking, and learning loops can all wait.

**Prioritization Results:**

- **Top Priority Ideas:** Studio Quote Builder, editable template library, polished quote preview, and quote-to-invoice conversion.
- **Quick Win Opportunities:** Thin client records, believable settings/defaults, accepted state, and preview-driven demo flow.
- **Breakthrough Concepts:** Studio Quote Builder as the product center, stacked multi-template proposals, mostly quote-led billing, and a thin but real persisted E2E milestone.
