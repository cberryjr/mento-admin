Six directions for a sharper quote workflow.

These mockups explore different ways to make quote preparation feel fast, direct, calm, and professional. Every direction uses the same product strategy: neutral Geist-like structure, restrained green accents, desktop-first workflow, and a focused workspace instead of a bloated dashboard.

How to review

  • Look for the direction that makes quote creation feel most obvious.
  • Notice where the client, template, line items, and preview sit in the hierarchy.
  • Compare whether a direction feels more like a workspace, a builder, or a workflow assistant.
  • Watch how the green accent is used: signal only, progress cue, or stronger call-to-action emphasis.
  • Choose a full direction or combine pieces, such as one layout with another navigation or preview treatment.

Recommended review questions

  • Which direction makes the path from new quote to preview feel most obvious?
  • Which layout best supports the future flow: quote, client, preview, send, accept, deposit, invoice?
  • How much density feels right for repeat work without turning into admin clutter?
  • Should preview be a validation side panel, a live split view, or the visual hero of the whole product?
  • Do you want the strongest emphasis on calm workspace, guided flow, or professional presentation?
